To make ScanGuard a professional, "Enterprise-Ready" product, you must implement a robust Data Lifecycle Management strategy. This isn't just about a "Delete" button; it's about legal compliance (GDPR/India's DPDP Act) and maintaining the "Security Brain" of your app.
Here is the high-level logic for handling scan history and account deletion:
1. Scan History Deletion: "Soft Delete" vs. "Hard Delete"
In a security app, you have a conflict: the user wants privacy, but your "Security Brain" needs data to detect scams.
The "Soft Delete" Logic (Recommended):
How it works: When a user clicks "Delete" on a scan, you don't actually erase the data from the database. Instead, you add a field: isDeleted: true.
The User View: The scan disappears from their history instantly. They feel in control.
The Business View: You keep the "Scam URL" and "VPA Handle" in your database for your Threat Intelligence Engine. You just "unlink" it from the user's visible profile.
Why this adds Value: You keep the data that makes your app smart without violating the user's privacy.
The "Hard Delete" Logic:
How it works: The record is physically erased from the database.
The Risk: If a user scans a dangerous phishing link and then deletes it, your app "forgets" that the link was dangerous. This weakens your global security network.
2. Account Deletion: The "Cascading Clean-Up"
When a user decides to leave ScanGuard, you must perform a "Cascading Delete" to ensure no "PII" (Personally Identifiable Information) is left behind.
Step 1: The "Grace Period" (The Big Platform Move):
Like Instagram or Facebook, don't delete immediately. Mark the account as status: "pending_deletion". Give the user 14 or 30 days to "Undo" the action. This prevents "Regret Deletions" and accidental data loss.
Step 2: The Data Wipe:
Once the grace period ends, your system must find every document tied to that userId.
Users Collection: Delete the profile (Name, Email, Photo).
Scans Collection: Delete all scans associated with that ID.
Username Release: If you have a unique username system, you must decide if that name becomes available for someone else to use.
Step 3: The "Anonymization" Alternative:
Instead of deleting the scans, you can "Anonymize" them. You delete the userId field from the scan record but keep the url and vpa.
The Result: The user is gone (Privacy), but the "Security Intelligence" stays (Business Value).
3. Why this is a "Legal Shield" for You
The App Store and Google Play Store now MANDATE that every app with an account system must have a "Delete Account" button inside the app.
The "Prison" Fear Fix: By having a clear "Delete Account" flow, you are complying with Data Privacy Laws. If a regulator asks, "What happens to user data?" you can show them your "Cascading Delete" logic. This proves you are not a "Data Hoarder" and are acting in good faith.
The "Right to be Forgotten": This is a core part of modern law. Implementing this makes your source code worth more because it is "Legally Compliant" out of the box.
4. Final Business Verdict on Data Deletion:
Does this make the app "Cheaper"? NO.
It makes it "Professional." A ₹20 Lakh app must have these "Boring but Important" features.
How to explain this to KSUM/Investors:
"ScanGuard follows Privacy-by-Design principles. We provide users with full control over their data, including 'Soft Delete' for history and a 'Cascading Wipe' for account deletion, ensuring we comply with global data protection standards like GDPR and India's DPDP Act."
My Advice:
Use Soft Deletes for individual scans (to keep your security data).
Use Hard Deletes for full account removal (to stay legally safe).
