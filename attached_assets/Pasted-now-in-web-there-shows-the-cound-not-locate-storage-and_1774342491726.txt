This error log provides a very clear picture of why your app is crashing. Here is the analysis of both the Android and Web errors.
1. The Android Error: CameraUnavailableException
What the error says:
The most important line is: Device reporting less cameras than anticipated... Available cameras: 0.
Why is this happening?
The Android system is telling your app: "You are asking me to start the camera, but I don't see any cameras on this device."
There are three main reasons for this:
Broken Hardware: If this is a real phone, the camera hardware might be physically damaged or disconnected internally. The app tries to "talk" to the camera, the hardware doesn't respond, and the app crashes because it doesn't know what to do with a "zero camera" result.
Emulator Issues: If you are testing on a computer (Emulator), the virtual camera is likely turned off in the settings. The app expects a camera, the emulator reports "0," and the app fails.
Camera "Lock": Sometimes another app (like WhatsApp or the system Camera app) is using the camera in the background. Android only allows one app to control the camera at a time. If the camera is "locked" by another app, it might report as "unavailable" to yours.
Why the crash occurs:
Your code is likely programmed to check for Permissions (asking the user for "Yes"), but it is not checking for Availability (asking the phone "Do you have a working camera?"). Because the app assumes a camera must be there, it crashes when it finds nothing.
2. The Web Error: "Could not locate storage"
What the error means:
On a website, "storage" usually refers to localStorage or IndexedDB—the place where the app saves your settings or temporary data.
Why is this happening?
Private/Incognito Mode: Many browsers block "storage" access when you are in a Private or Incognito window to protect your privacy.
Iframe Restrictions: If your web app is running inside another window (like a preview or a "sandbox"), the browser often blocks storage access for security reasons.
Browser Settings: The user might have "Block all cookies and site data" turned on in their browser settings.
The Solution (Conceptually)
To fix these issues without the app crashing, the logic needs to change from "Force" to "Check":
The "Safety Check" (Android): Before the app even tries to show the camera screen, it should ask the phone: "How many cameras do you have?" If the answer is 0, the app should skip the camera entirely and show a message like: "No camera detected. Please upload your QR code from the gallery instead." This prevents the crash because the app never tries to start the broken hardware.
The "Fallback" (Web): For the storage error, the app should check if storage is "locked." If it is, instead of failing, it should save data to the phone's temporary memory (RAM). It won't save your settings forever, but it will stop the app from crashing.
