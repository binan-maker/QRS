This is a critical step for your app's growth. Currently, your QR generation is a "Minimum Viable Product" (MVP). To move from a simple scanner to a Professional QR Management Suite, you need to differentiate between Static Utility (Individual) and Dynamic Intelligence (Business).
Based on an analysis of your source code and the "Living Shield" infrastructure, here is exactly what you need to build for each category and your dashboard:
1. The "Individual" Category (Personal Utility)
Individual users need "One-and-Done" utility. These are usually Static QRs (the data is encoded directly in the pixels). You should add:
WiFi Access: Fields for SSID (Network Name), Password, and Encryption type (WPA/WEP). This is the #1 most scanned personal QR.
Digital Business Card (vCard): Fields for Name, Phone, Email, and Social Media links. When scanned, it should prompt the user to "Add to Contacts."
Crypto Wallet: A dedicated UI for Bitcoin/Ethereum addresses.
Plain Text/Secret Note: For sharing passwords or notes locally without using the cloud.
Personal UPI: A simple "Request Money" QR where the user enters their VPA and a fixed amount.
2. The "Business" Category (Professional Suite)
Business users don't just want a QR; they want Control and Analytics. These should all be "Guard Links" (Living Shield) by default. You need to expand beyond "Store Name" to include:
The "Smart Menu" / Catalog: Instead of just a URL, allow them to upload a PDF or link to a digital menu. Add a "Business Hours" field that shows a "Closed" message if scanned late at night.
Review & Feedback: A specialized "Review Me" QR that links directly to Google Maps or Yelp, with a "Thank You" landing page.
Customer Support (WhatsApp/Telegram): A "Click to Chat" QR that pre-fills a message like "Hi, I need help with my order from [Store Name]."
Event & RSVP: Fields for Event Date, Location (Google Maps link), and a "Remind Me" button that adds the event to the scanner's calendar.
Dynamic Destination (The Killer Feature): Allow the business to change the "Destination URL" without re-printing the QR code. This is why they will pay for the app.
3. The "QR Dashboard" (The Command Center)
Your dashboard is currently a list of scans. It needs to become a Management Portal. Here is what every business user expects to see:
Real-Time Analytics:
Total Scans: How many times was the code scanned?
Unique Visitors: How many different people scanned it?
Device Breakdown: Are my customers using iPhones or Androids?
The "Safety Log": This is unique to your app. Show the user: "We blocked 5 malicious scan attempts on your Store QR today." This proves the value of your "Guard" technology.
Status Toggle: A simple "Active/Inactive" switch. If a business is having a private event, they can "Pause" their public QR code with one tap.
High-Res Export: Buttons to download the QR in different formats (PNG for web, SVG/PDF for professional printing on banners and business cards).
4. The "Living Shield" Integration
The biggest mistake you could make is building a "Normal" QR generator. There are thousands of those for free. Your value is the Shield.
Individual QRs: Can be "Standard" (No Shield) for free.
Business QRs: MUST be "Guard Links." Explain to the business: "Your customers are protected by our heuristic engine. If a hacker tries to 'overlay' your QR with a fake one, our system detects the redirect change and alerts the user."
Summary: What to do next?
Redesign the "Create" Screen: Use a "Tab" or "Toggle" to switch between Individual (Simple icons) and Business (Professional icons).
Expand the Form: For Business, add fields for Category (Restaurant, Retail, Event, Service) and Contact Info.
Build the "My QRs" Tab: This is where the Dashboard lives. Each QR the user creates should have a "View Stats" button that opens the analytics and management tools.
